# Physical AI Architecture: From Cloud to Edge

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

-   **Diagram the 3-tier architecture** (Workstation/Cloud, Edge, Robot) and explain the role of each layer.
-   **Explain the Sim-to-Real workflow**, identifying where simulation ends and reality begins.
-   **Identify the data flow** from sensors to actuators and why bandwidth constraints dictate architecture.
-   **Verify network connectivity** between your workstation and edge device.
-   **Execute a cross-device ROS 2 application** to prove your "nervous system" is functional.

## Why Your Laptop Isn't Enough

In traditional software engineering, you write code on a laptop, deploy it to a server (AWS/Azure), and users access it via a browser. The "server" has infinite power, and the "client" is just a display.

**Robotics is different.**

A robot is a server, a client, and a physics engine all rolled into one battery-powered package. It cannot rely on the cloud for its reflexesâ€”if your internet connection drops for 500ms, a chatbot just pauses, but a walking robot falls over and breaks a $2,000 motor.

This necessitates a distributed architecture where intelligence is split between:
1.  **Training & Simulation** (Infinite Compute, Offline)
2.  **Inference & Control** (Limited Compute, Real-time)

To build this, we need specific hardware.

:::danger Hardware Requirement
**Standard laptops will NOT work for this chapter's simulation components.**

To follow the "Digital Twin" portions of this course, you **MUST** have a workstation with:
-   **NVIDIA RTX 4070 Ti (12GB VRAM)** or higher.
-   **Ubuntu 22.04 LTS** (Native or distinct drive, WSL2 is experimental for GUI apps).

For the "Physical Twin" portions, you need:
-   **NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano** (8GB) or Orin NX.
:::
